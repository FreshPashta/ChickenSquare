Chicken Square

A backyard chicken reference app designed just for you, Kaubs!

Feel free to share with any chicken owners.

What this does

  • Separates broad respiratory disease from more specific patterns like HPAI / Newcastle, IBV, ILT, MG/MS, and coryza.
  • Uses age and bird type to shift diagnoses such as coccidiosis, avian encephalomyelitis, Marek’s, leukosis, and reproductive disorders.
  • Adds management issues like pasty butt, sour crop, vent gleet, parasites, frostbite, bullying, and bumblefoot.

How to use it

1. Enter details

  • Select bird type and age.
  • Choose flock context and symptom combinations.
  • Use the additional signs that make one disease pattern different from another.

2. Review ranking

  • See urgency separately from likely differential patterns.
  • Read why a pattern ranked high.
  • See which clues matter most next.

3. Learn and track

  • Use the care library for general chicken education.
  • Keep browser-only history for repeat checks.
  • Print a result summary if needed.

Devon's Chicken Squares

A recipe for the best Chicken Squares!

Makes about 8 squares Bake at 350°F Bake time: 35–40 minutes

Ingredients

  • 8 oz cream cheese
  • 2 tbsp butter, plus extra melted butter for brushing
  • 4 cups cubed cooked chicken, preferably chicken breasts
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp pepper
  • 2 to 4 tbsp milk
  • 2 tbsp chopped chives
  • 2 cans crescent rolls
  • Crushed cornflakes, enough to coat

Instructions

  1. Cream together the cream cheese and 2 tablespoons of butter until smooth.
  2. Mix in the cubed chicken, salt, pepper, 2 to 4 tablespoons of milk, and chopped chives until well combined.
  3. Open the 2 cans of crescent rolls and divide each can into 4 rectangle pieces.
  4. Use your fingers to smooth together the angled perforations so each piece forms a solid rectangle.
  5. Divide the chicken mixture evenly onto the dough pieces.
  6. Pull the 4 corners of each rectangle up to the top, twist to seal, and press the edges together well.
  7. Brush each square with melted butter.
  8. Lift each square with a spatula and place it into crushed cornflakes, coating well by tossing cornflakes over the top and pressing them in.
  9. Place the coated squares on an ungreased cookie sheet.
  10. Bake at 350°F for 35 to 40 minutes, or until golden brown.

Recipe notes

  • Use just enough milk to make the filling creamy and easy to scoop.
  • Chicken breasts are preferred, but any cooked chicken cut into small cubes should work.
  • Press the crescent dough seams together well so the filling stays enclosed while baking.

Sources

These are the main sources used for the current care content, triage logic, and disease pattern matching in this version of the app.

USDA APHIS — Defend the Flock: Signs of Illness

Core flock illness warning signs including sudden death, breathing changes, decreased egg production, swelling, purple discoloration, neurologic signs, and diarrhea.

USDA APHIS — Virulent Newcastle Disease

Used for severe respiratory and neurologic outbreak-style patterning such as torticollis, gasping, watery diarrhea, and high flock concern.

USDA APHIS — Avian Influenza

Used for avian influenza signs, flock-level concern logic, and the importance of sudden deaths, respiratory distress, and rapid spread.

USDA APHIS — How to Protect Your Flock from Avian Influenza

Used for biosecurity, monitoring, and outbreak-response guidance referenced in the app.

Merck Veterinary Manual — Common Infectious Diseases in Backyard Poultry

Primary reference for disease group differentiation including Marek’s disease, avian encephalomyelitis, infectious bronchitis, fowlpox, leukosis, and colibacillosis context.

Merck Veterinary Manual — Common Management-Related Problems in Backyard Poultry

Used for noninfectious and management-linked concerns such as trauma, flock management issues, and common backyard problem framing.

Merck Veterinary Manual — Management of Backyard Poultry

Used for general care, mixed-age flock considerations, heat stress, housing, and nutrition-related guidance.

Merck Veterinary Manual — Mycoplasma gallisepticum Infection in Poultry

Used for chronic respiratory disease and “bubbly eye” style pattern matching.

University of Minnesota Extension — Avian Influenza Basics for Noncommercial Poultry Flock Owners

Used for separation of new birds, wild bird exposure risk, and flock biosecurity practices.

UNH Extension — Brooding and Caring for Chicks

Used for chick care, brooder heat guidance, and young-bird education content.
